How Do I File a Wrongful Death Claim?

If you believe negligence or a wrongful act might have caused the death of your loved one, you need to get in touch with a personal injury attorney as soon as possible after their passing. Your memory of the events that led to their death will be fresher, and you won’t risk missing the statute of limitations deadline. Usually, close relatives, such as spouses and next of kin, have the right to file wrongful death claims. If you’re a spouse or next of kin, making an appointment with one of our personal injury lawyers is the first step toward justice. Get in touch with our caring team today.

What Kinds of Compensation Might a Wrongful Death Settlement Provide?

A successful wrongful death claim can provide surviving family members with at least some of the wages and benefits they would likely have earned in the future, money to pay medical and funeral bills, and possibly punitive damages, should the situation warrant it. Additional damages might include monetary compensation for the loss of companionship, pain and suffering, and other losses your loved one’s death brought you. Let one of our wrongful death attorneys sit down with you and your family to see if a wrongful death claim is right for you. Make an appointment today.
